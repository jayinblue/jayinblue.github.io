wikiHow: Growling on the clarinet

How to growl on the clarinet

Growling has a long history in jazz and street perfomances. It can also be considered flutter tonguing, as the technique is the same. Here is a short tutorial on how to perform this technique on your clarinet easily.

1

Step 1: Getting a baseline

Pick a note to practice your growl on. Good starting notes are the various throat tones on the clarinet, for example. Pick a note you are personally comfortable playing at a stable volume with good air support.

2

Step 2: The technique sans clarinet

Without the clarinet, practice humming, making a gargling sound, or even a "Spanish R" sound. Find one that is comfortable for you to perform, don't worry about sound at this step.

3

Step 3: Putting it together

Now, playing the note you picked in step 1, practice performing the noise you practiced in step 2. It takes time to develop, almost like doing two things at once. Try to keep a constant air stream going through the instrument while you experiment with different sounds to find what is comfortable for you.

4

Step 4: Going beyond

Growling can add interesting texture to simple music, however be careful! You don't want to add unnecessary noise to a classical song, for example. Experimenting with different pitches to hum can also give different effects. A common pitch is a perfect 5th above the note you're playing, however you can experiment and find what works and sounds good to you.
